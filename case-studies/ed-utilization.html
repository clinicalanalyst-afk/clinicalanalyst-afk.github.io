Overview

This case study analyzes Emergency Department (ED) utilization patterns using publicly available data. The goal of this project was to identify trends in patient volume, peak times, staffing needs, and opportunities to improve resource allocation in hospital emergency settings.

Objectives

  • Identify patterns in patient arrival times and volume
  • Evaluate potential factors influencing ED overcrowding
  • Provide recommendations to improve throughput and reduce wait times

Tools & Techniques

  • Excel – Cleaning, filtering, pivot tables, trend charts
  • SQL – Subsetting and summarizing utilization queries
  • Data visualization – Communicating findings clearly

Key Findings

  • Significant increase in ED utilization during late afternoons and weekends
  • Higher volumes among certain age categories and repeat visitors
  • Identified time blocks where staffing adjustments would improve throughput

Conclusion

This analysis provides a foundation for operational improvements in ED settings. It demonstrates the ability to interpret healthcare utilization data, identify patterns, and turn insights into data-driven recommendations.
